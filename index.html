01 Authority stays at the edge

01 / Belief

The safest central keeper
is no central keeper.

The internet taught us to rent our identity from platforms. We are working on the opposite: cryptographic authority that begins with people, is shared only by deliberate consent, and remains useful even when a service disappears.

02 / Mission

Make digital ownership
practical, not theoretical.

01

Move trust to your devices

Your approved devices hold the authority. There is no universal account password waiting to be phished.

02

Keep storage replaceable

Clouds and servers should carry encrypted data—not own the keys or decide who gets access.

03

Expose the machinery

Security deserves inspection. Our products and cryptographic foundations are built in the open.

04 / Principles

What we refuse
to compromise.

01

Keys before accounts

Identity should be proven cryptographically, not borrowed from a central user database.

02

Consent before access

New devices and sensitive actions should require visible, deliberate authorization.

03

Ciphertext beyond the edge

Secrets are encrypted before they leave the client. Storage providers see encrypted material.

04

Recovery without a back door

Resilience should come from devices, trusted participants, or keys you choose—not universal recovery access.

05 / The horizon

A future where losing a platform does not mean losing yourself.

We imagine digital identity as portable personal infrastructure: devices can prove authority, encrypted data can move between storage providers, and people can coordinate trust without placing every secret under one operator. Meta Secret is building toward that future one usable vault at a time.
